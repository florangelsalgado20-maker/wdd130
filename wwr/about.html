Rafting Company Name

Our story begins not with a grand launch, but with a global pause. In 2019, the seeds of Down the River were planted by a small group of experienced river guides and outdoor educators who shared a common dream: to create expeditions that were less about checklist tourism and more about deep, transformative connection with wild waterways. Then, in 2020, the world stopped. The pandemic forced everyone indoors, severing our connection to the rivers we loved. But it was in this forced stillness that our purpose crystallized. We saw a growing, profound hunger for raw, authentic nature, for spaces of freedom and resilience away from crowded places. The name "Down the River" emerged as a metaphor for surrender—to the flow of the current, to the uncertainty of the times, and to the powerful healing journey that only a river can provide.

History

In the mid-19th century, businessman Don Martín Ávila purchased a large hacienda in the mountains north of Caracas. The property contained a stunning natural waterfall that cascaded down a cliff face from the Ávila Mountain. Captivated by its beauty and entrepreneurial spirit, Don Martín had the visionary idea to create a resort for the capital's elite. Around 1874, he opened "El Salto" (The Waterfall), an ambitious and luxurious recreational complex. It featured elegant paths, viewing balconies carved into the rock for admiring the 30-meter fall, gardens, and even a small tramway to transport visitors. For decades, it was the premier getaway for wealthy caraqueños, a symbol of elegance and nature just a short trip from the city.
